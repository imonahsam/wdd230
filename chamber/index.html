THE EXECUTIVE SECRETARY OF THE BOARD OF TECHNICAL AND VOCATIONAL EDUCATION AND TRAINING (TVET) VISITS BENIN CHAMBER OF COMMERCE EXECUTIVE COUNCIL

On Tuesday, April 23rd, 2024, an anticipated meeting is scheduled between The President, Hon. (Barr.) Austin Atakpu, and select Council members, who are set to host Dr. Terseer Nyulaku, the Executive Secretary of the Board of Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET), along with his team. The venue for this engagement is the President's office located in GRA, Benin City.

The formalities will commence with introductions from both sides. Dr. Nyulaku will highlight that the involvement of private sector entities is crucial not only due to their status as employers but also because of their integral position as social partners in the TVET ecosystem.

During the discourse, Dr. Terseer Nyulaku will express the intention of his team to seek partnership with BENCCIMA, particularly in light of the forthcoming launch of the Edo State Technical Talent Development Policy. He will assure the Chamber of their commitment to collaboration by promising to forward the draft policy document for their valuable input and feedback.

INTRODUCTION

The Benin Chamber of Commerce was incorporated on 20th October 1965, in Nigeria as a non-profit making organization, limited by guarantee (without a share capital) with company registration number RC 4602/T. The Chamber has produced 15 Presidents.

The Chamber of Commerce and Industry of Benin (CCIB) is supervised by the Ministry of Trade and is responsible for representing, promoting, and protecting the interests of economic operators. CCIB also regulates certain sectors of the economy. The site provides information about CCIB and its services, links to CCIB partners, news reports, as well as information on investing, events, and general tourist information on the country. Only available in French.
